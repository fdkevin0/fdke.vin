**The Beijing News | Must students send holiday greetings to student cadres? Sichuan University of Science and Engineering says it will investigate seriously**

The Beijing News reported that on October 2, a netizen posted on Weibo saying that student cadres in the student union at Sichuan University of Science and Engineering required members to send holiday greetings to other cadres, and that getting a name wrong led to punishment: copying the correct name fifty times and being criticized at a large meeting. That same night, the university's official microblog issued a statement saying it had received complaints about inappropriate language from student cadres and had already begun an investigation.

Screenshots from a group chat showed that a student surnamed Ye, in a QQ group called "2018 Student Union Organization Department", told members to send Mid-Autumn Festival greetings to other ministers and the presidium so as to "show the style of our department". The message also warned members to be careful with forms of address: "I don't want other ministers telling me that one of our junior members typed a name wrong or called someone 'minister so-and-so', especially members of the presidium."

Other screenshots showed a new department member sending Mid-Autumn greetings to a student cadre and then being told to copy the recipient's name fifty times and make a self-criticism at a meeting. The screenshot was also posted back into the department group, where another cadre scolded the student: "We have repeated the name and gender many times today, why are you still making this mistake?" The same person added that anyone making such mistakes again would be punished by copying the student-club management rules and the student-union constitution.

At around 11 p.m. that night, the university's official account released a statement saying that the school had already received complaints concerning inappropriate language used by student cadres in their work. The university party committee attached great importance to the matter, would investigate seriously and carefully, and would later release the result to the public.
